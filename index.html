      Content Guidelines

      What makes a great learning video on Duolingo.

      What scores high

      • Clear, natural speech at a steady pace
      • Accurate subtitles in the target language
      • Real-world scenarios (ordering food, asking directions)
      • Vocabulary that matches the tagged level
      • Repetition of key phrases
      • Visual context that supports comprehension
      • Under 60 seconds for maximum engagement

      What lowers your score

      • Heavy background music over speech
      • Too much slang without explanation
      • Speaking too fast for the tagged level
      • No subtitles or auto-generated only
      • Mixing multiple languages without context
      • Content unrelated to language learning
      • Poor audio quality or echo

      Pro tips from top creators

      • Start with a hook: "Here's how to say ___ in Spanish"
      • Show the word/phrase on screen while saying it
      • Use a "repeat after me" moment for engagement
      • End with a quick quiz or prompt
      • Film in the real environment (cafe, market, street)
      • Tag accurately — wrong level = bad match = low retention

      How the Learning Score works

      Every video gets an automated 0–100 score based on these factors:

      Speech Clarity — 30%
      Subtitle Quality — 20%
      Vocabulary Fit — 20%
      Speaking Speed — 15%
      Repetition & Phrases — 10%
      Slang / Noise Penalty — 5%
